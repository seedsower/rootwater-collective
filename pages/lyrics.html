TRACK 01 — RIVERS & ROOTS

Rivers Still Rising

KEY: Em BPM: 78 GENRE: Roots Reggae DURATION: 4:32
VERSE I

The river runs clean / when the frequency is pure
And the root holds deep / when the foundation secure
What the physicist measured / the mystic already told
Every part contains the whole

CHORUS

Rivers still rising / roots still holding

From the baobab tree / to the quantum field

VERSE II

They said the universe expanding / but I feel it contract
Down to this moment / this breath / this track
Marley sang it in seventy-three / science proved it in eleven
The same frequency / earth to heaven

BRIDGE

Listen to the silence / between the one and the drop
That's where the universe / remembers how to stop
And start again / and start again
The river never ends / it just begins

CHORUS (REPRISE)

Rivers still rising / roots still holding
The water keeps flowing / the story keeps unfolding
From the baobab tree / to the quantum field
What was hidden / is now revealed

Production Note Opening track. Sets the frequency. Analog dub delays in D major over E minor root — intentional tension that resolves in the chorus. Live horns recorded in a single take. The "river" is both metaphor and method: the bassline flows continuously without break for the entire 4:32, representing the eternal current. One-drop riddim with emphasis on the third beat — the cosmological moment of pause before motion resumes.

TRACK 02 — RIVERS & ROOTS

Theory of Everything

KEY: Am BPM: 72 GENRE: Roots Reggae / Dub DURATION: 5:18
VERSE I

Every part contains the whole
The physicist proved / what the mystic already know
From the atom to the galaxy / same pattern, same flow
What Marley sang / what Einstein tried to show

CHORUS

This is the theory of everything
Hidden in a riddim / wrapped in a string
From the drum to the cosmos / one continuous ring
Listen to the frequency / hear what it sing

VERSE II

Babylon build walls / but the frequency pass through
They measure the universe / we already knew
The boundary between me and you / is a window not a wall
When the bassline drop / we remember it all

Production Note Built on a single bass note (A) for the first two minutes. Patience as architecture. Drums enter at 2:14. Horns at 3:02. The space between beats is as important as the beats themselves — sacred geometry in the silence. Vocals recorded in one take with intentional imperfections left intact. The "mistakes" are proof of humanness, which is proof of divinity.

TRACK 01 — THE INNER MERIDIAN

The Mirror Opens

KEY: Dm BPM: 74 GENRE: Roots Reggae DURATION: 4:18
VERSE I

Look into the mirror / but don't look at your face
Look through it / look past it / look into space
The boundary is a window / not a wall at all
And when you stop knocking / the door was open all along

CHORUS

The mirror opens / when you stop trying to see
What you thought was reflection / is infinity
The meridian runs through you / ancient and new

Production Note Recorded in a single room with all musicians present. No headphones. No isolation. Pure acoustic bleed — you can hear every breath, every footstep, every moment of human presence. This is intentional. The imperfections are the perfection. The sound of being in the same space, breathing the same air, locked into the same frequency.
